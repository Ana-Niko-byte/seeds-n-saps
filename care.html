Common Problems

It can be quite alarming to look at one of your new green buddies and notice something doesn't look quite right.

Don't sweat it - more often than not, this is caused by something tiny, like smothering the plant with a bit too much motherly-love.

Good news, though! I've compiled a quick guide below on some of the most common plant problems, which should hopefully get your plant baby looking bright and shiny in no time!

(Hover over the Cards)

Root Rot?

Root rot is a very common problem, usually caused by overwatering, poor soil, or soil fungi.

Tips & Remedies

Carefully remove all rotting roots (except the main thick ones!), and leave the plant out of its pot to dry for a day.

After repotting (with a 4:1 ratio of original:new soil), be careful not to overwater it.

Brown Spots?

Brown spots are usually cause by overwatering, underwatering, and lack of humidity. Strange, right? Another cause can be direct sunlight.

Tips & Remedies

Brown spots are usually an indicator of some sort of fungal infection from overwatering.

Place away from direct sunlight and do not water your plant for a few days until the soil has dried.

Dry Leaves?

Dry leaves are usually caused by cold air, overwatering, or dry air - kind of like frostbite.

Tips & Remedies

To stop this problem from spreading to the rest of the leaves, move your plant into a room-temperature location and cut off the dead leaves.

Do not water your plant if the soil is moist - otherwise, use room-temperature water.

Tiny Insects?

Thrips are tiny black insects that feed on your plants and leave them looking dull, dry, and dreary. They typically arrive indoors from grocery shops.

Tips & Remedies

To get rid of thrips, you will need to purchase a special spray, found in most diy and plant stores.

Don't remove anything by hand as they can travel and infest other plants.

Mold?

Molding is typically caused by high humidity and low airflow, causing the plant to essentially 'rot'.

Tips & Remedies

This will vary depending on how deep the mold has gotten into the plant.

Remove as much of the mold as possible using a spoon, place the plant near a window so the soil dries out.

Here is a great in-depth guide for this problem.

Wilting?

Wilting is usually a sign of drought, or waterlogged soil.

Tips & Remedies

Check the soil for either a sign of drought, or a sign of waterlogging.

If soil is very dry, water the plant and place into shade. If the soil is wet, drain the soil and do not water for a few days.
