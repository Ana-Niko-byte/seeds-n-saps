Complete Care Guide for Beginners

Hi there!

My name is Ana, and I'm passionate about all things green!

I've had the pleasure of sharing my space with houseplants for as long as I can recall (they're practically a Belarusian household tradition). Plants are an essential asset to a cosy home, bringing life, movement, aesthetic value, and natural fragrances into homes.

As beginner plant-parents, choosing which plant to buy can be quite challenging, but fret not! I've designed a little guide below to help you choose your first plant!

Find out what makes us a great household addition!

(Hover over the Cards)

Cactii

Cactii and succulents are very beginner-friendly.

Aside from being low maintenance, they are able to withstand the toughest indoor conditions and rarely succumb to pest infections due to their hard exterior skins.

Buy a cactus here.

Snake Plant

Snake plants are very forgiving in their care routines, can survive in shadier areas of your home-link and are also very easy to propagate incase you decide you want more than one.

Buy a snake plant here.

Zz Plant

Zz plants are perfect for forgetful plant parents, and have evolved to survive even in low light, draughty and low humidity conditions.

Buy a zz-plant here.

Swiss Cheese Plant

Apart from looking very boozy, the swiss cheese plant is easy to care for and generally pest-free.

However, the leaves are quite fragile and are prone to wilting, sunburn and brown spots.

Buy a swiss cheese Plant here.

Rubber Fig

Rubber figs don't need a lot of watering and hardly ever need fertilising. Their leaves store water, making them an ideal addition to a busy household!

Buy a rubber fig here.

Jade Plant

The jade plant, also known as a Chinese money tree, does not need frequent watering and can withstand direct sunlight. This plant is also very easy to propagate incase you decide you want more than one in your home.

Buy a jade plant here.
