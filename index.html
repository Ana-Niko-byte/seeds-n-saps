Complete Care Guide for Beginners

Hi there!

My name is Ana, and I'm passionate about all things green!

I've had the pleasure of sharing my space with houseplants for as long as I can recall (they're practically a Belarusian household tradition). Plants are an essential asset to a cosy home, bringing life, movement, aesthetic value, and natural fragrances into homes.

As beginner plant-parents, choosing which plant to buy can be quite challenging, but fret not! I've designed a little guide below to help you choose your first plant!

Cactii

Cactii and succulents are extremely beginner-friendly, being low maintenance and able to withstand the toughest indoor conditions.

Snake Plant

Snake plants are very forgiving in their care routines, and can be easily propagated for more snake baby plants!

Zz Plant

Zz plants are perfect for forgetful plant parents, and have evolved to survive even in low light, draughty and low humidity conditions.

Swiss Cheese Plant

Apart from looking very boozy, the swiss cheese plant is easy to care for and generally pest-free. However, this is the hardest out of the bunch.

Rubber Fig

Rubber figs don't need a lot of watering and hardly ever need fertilising. Their leaves store water, making them an ideal addition to a busy household!

Jade Plant

The jade plant, also known as a Chinese money tree, does not need frequent watering and can withstand direct sunlight.
